555th Parachute Infantry Battalion · Age 108

One of the last surviving Triple Nickle veterans, Sgt. Harris carried the memory of the battalion for more than eight decades. He spoke at schools, ceremonies, and chapter meetings — always with the same quiet dignity he carried into every jump. He is irreplaceable.

February 25th

The Day the 555th Became a Battalion

On February 25, 1944, the 555th Parachute Infantry Company was redesignated the 555th Parachute Infantry Battalion at Camp Mackall, North Carolina — making it the first and only all-Black parachute battalion in American military history.

Under the command of Lt. Col. John Warren, the 555th began training for combat deployment. What they couldn't have known was that their assignment would take them not to Europe or the Pacific, but to the forests of Oregon and Washington — where Japanese balloon bombs had set the Pacific Northwest on fire.

That deployment — Operation Firefly — would prove their excellence in ways combat never could, and set the stage for the integration of the U.S. Armed Forces just three years later.
