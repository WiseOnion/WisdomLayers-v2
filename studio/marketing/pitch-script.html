WisdomLayers — Pitch Script

In-person sales guide · Keep this with you · Read it until it's natural

The story is the pitch. You're not just selling a website — you're a group of high school students working hard to pay your way through college. People want to help young people who are putting in the work. Lead with that. It's not a gimmick, it's the truth — and the truth sells.
1
The Opener — Walk in, get their attention
~30 sec
You say
"Hi, my name is Wisdom — I'm not trying to take up too much of your time. I'm a high school student and I run a small web design company called WisdomLayers with a group of my classmates. We're all working to pay our way through college, and we build professional websites for local businesses like yours. Do you have about 60 seconds? I'd love to show you what we do."
Body language: Smile, make eye contact, hold the card in your hand so they can see it. Don't shove it at them — wait until they say yes.
2
The Hook — Make them feel the problem
~45 sec
If they say yes, continue
"Most small businesses have a website that's either outdated, super slow, or just doesn't look the way the business actually is. And the problem is — when someone looks you up online, that's their first impression. If the site doesn't look professional, they assume the business isn't either. We fix that. We build clean, fast, modern websites that actually make people want to call you."
Pause here. Let it land. Then hand them the card.
3
The Show — Hand them something
~30 sec
Hand them the card and say
"This is our work — you can check out wisdomlayers.com to see the actual sites we've built. We've done a therapy practice, a pressure washing company, and we have more in the works. Everything is custom — we don't use templates. And we keep our prices fair because we're building our portfolio and we genuinely want to help local businesses win online."
4
The Ask — Close without pressure
~20 sec
Then ask
"Is your current website something you're happy with? Or is it something you've been meaning to update?"
Shut up after you ask. Let them answer. Don't fill the silence. Whatever they say, listen fully before responding.
If they're interested, say
"Awesome — the best next step is just a quick free call where I ask you some questions about your business and we figure out what makes sense. No commitment at all. Can I get your email or number so I can send you a time that works?"
!
Handling Objections
They say: "We already have a website."
"That's great — honestly most of our clients did too. The question is whether it's actually bringing people in or just sitting there. Would you be open to a quick look together? No pressure either way."
They say: "We don't have the budget right now."
"Totally understand. We work in a 50/50 payment split — half upfront, half when it's done. But even if the timing isn't right, take our info for when it is."
They say: "How old are you guys?"
"We're in high school — but our work isn't. Pull up wisdomlayers.com right now and judge us by what we build, not our age. Our clients trusted us for the same reason."
They say: "I need to talk to my partner/boss."
"Of course — here's our flyer so you have all the info. If it's easier, I can send a quick email you can both review together. What's the best email?"
5
Before You Leave
Left them the hand card (front/back)
Left them the full flyer if they seemed interested
Mentioned wisdomlayers.com at least twice
Got their name, email, or number — OR confirmed they'll reach out
Thanked them genuinely regardless of their answer
Left on a positive note — you may come back someday
"We're not just selling websites. We're funding our future."
