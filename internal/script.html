Sales Script
WisdomLayers — In-Person
Step 1 of 7
Walk Up

The opener.

Say this
"Hey, I'm Wisdom — I run a web design studio called WisdomLayers. I build custom websites for local businesses. Do you guys have a website right now?"
If yes
"Cool — how's it working for you? Are people finding you on Google, or is it mostly word of mouth?"
If no
"Okay perfect, that's actually where I come in."
Keep it casual. You're starting a conversation, not a pitch.
iPad stays in your bag at this point.
Step 2 of 7
Hand Over the Card

The hook.

Front of card — say this
"So this is us — WisdomLayers. We build websites specifically for local businesses like yours. Not templates, not Wix — fully custom, built to actually bring people in the door."
Flip to the back — say this
"The way it works is simple — three steps. We hop on a quick call, we build your site around your brand, and we launch it. You're live in 2 to 3 weeks."
Hand it front side first, let them look, then flip it yourself.
Don't rush. Let the card do work while you talk.
Step 3 of 7
Make It About Them

The problem.

Say this
"Honestly, most people are gonna Google you before they ever walk through that door. If your site looks rough or you don't have one at all — they're already clicking on somebody else. That's money walking away."
Pause after this. Let it land. Don't fill the silence.
Make it specific — swap in their business type. "Before they order fried turkey from you" lands harder than something generic.
Step 4 of 7
Pull Out the iPad

Show the work.

iPad Open wisdomlayers.com/sales.html — scroll to Our Work section
Tap Miles2Wisdom — say this
"This is a therapy practice we built — full booking system, team page, the whole thing. Live and running."
Tap PJ Pressure — say this
"This one's a pressure washing company — local service business just like yours. They get leads straight from the site now."
If they're a food business
"We've built for restaurants too — menu pages, order info, hours, all of it. Every site is built from scratch for that specific business."
Don't read off the screen. Point at things and talk naturally.
Let them hold the iPad if they want to — that's a good sign.
Step 5 of 7
Scroll to Pricing

The numbers.

iPad Scroll down to the Pricing section — let them read it first
Say this
"Pricing is all right here, nothing hidden. Most local businesses go with the Professional package — that's $1,499. You get 5 to 7 pages, a contact or booking form, SEO set up so Google can actually find you, the whole thing."
If budget seems tight
"If you just want something simple to get started, the Landing Page is $599. One page, looks clean, gets people to call you."
Say the price confidently and stop talking. Don't justify it immediately.
Let them sit with the screen. Silence is not awkward — it means they're reading.
Step 6 of 7
Handle the Pushback

The objections.

If they say "I need to think about it"
"No pressure at all — that's literally on our card. But I do offer a free consultation, zero commitment. We just talk about what you'd need, I tell you exactly what I'd build, and you decide from there."
If they ask "why you over someone else"
"Because you're gonna talk directly to me — not some account manager. I'm invested in this because this is how I'm building my future. Every site I build has my name on it. I don't cut corners."
If they say "that's too expensive"
"I hear you. The Landing Page at $599 is a solid starting point — gets you online, looks professional, and starts bringing people in. We can always build it out later."
If they say "we already have a website"
"That's cool — can I ask, is it mobile-friendly? A lot of sites look fine on a computer but break on phones, and that's where most people are searching from now."
Never argue. Agree, then redirect.
The free consultation is your pressure release valve — always have it ready.
Step 7 of 7
Seal It

The close.

Say this
"Can I get your number or email? I'll reach out this week and we can set up that free call — 15 minutes, that's all it takes."
If they won't give contact info
"No worries — just go to wisdomlayers.com, there's a contact form right on the site. Goes straight to me."

Free consultation. No pressure.

That's the close. One ask — number, email, or site visit. Not all three.

Leave the card face-up on the back side when you walk away.
One ask at the close. Don't give them three options — pick the one that fits the vibe.
Even if they don't commit, you planted a seed. They have your card.
