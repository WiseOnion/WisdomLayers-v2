Thank You for Choosing WisdomLayers

We appreciate the opportunity to serve you and are committed to delivering a professional website that accurately represents your brand. This document outlines the project process, your responsibilities, and what you can expect at each stage of development.

WisdomLayers is committed to clear communication, quality craftsmanship, and results that meet your business objectives.

Your project officially commences upon receipt of your signed Web Development Agreement and confirmed deposit payment.

Project Process — Seven-Step Overview

1

Execute Agreement and Submit Deposit

Return your signed Web Development Agreement along with the 50% deposit payment. Upon receipt and confirmation of both, your project is officially queued and the process advances to Step 2 within one business day.

2

Content Collection

Content collection begins immediately after the discovery call. Submit all project materials — written copy, photographs, logo, and brand assets — directly by email to contact@wisdomlayers.com. Note: The project build timeline does not begin until all content is received. Delays in content submission will extend the estimated delivery date accordingly.

3

Discovery Call (30 to 45 Minutes)

A scheduled video call will be conducted to align on your vision, brand identity, target audience, design preferences, and required features. This call informs the direction of the entire project. Clients are encouraged to prepare notes in advance.

4

Development and Progress Check-Ins

Once all content is on file, the website build begins. Structured progress updates are provided at key milestones. All feedback and revision requests must be submitted in writing by email to contact@wisdomlayers.com.

5

Preview and Revision Window

A preview link is shared for your review. You have 10 business days to submit all revision requests in writing by email. Revisions within the agreed scope are included. Requests submitted after this window or outside original scope are billed as additional work.

6

Launch Approval and Final Invoice

Once revisions are complete and you are satisfied, a Launch Approval Form is sent for your written sign-off. Upon receipt, the Final Invoice (remaining 50%) is issued. No website will be deployed prior to confirmed receipt of full payment.

7

Final Payment and Launch

Upon receipt and clearance of the final payment (remaining 50%), the website will be deployed to the Client's hosting environment. The post-launch support period begins on the date of launch. No website will be launched prior to confirmed receipt of full payment.

Required Materials

Content Checklist — Items Required Before Development Begins

Written Copy — Final or near-final text content for all pages (Home, About, Services, Contact, etc.). A Google Document or Microsoft Word file is preferred. Placeholder text is not acceptable and will delay project commencement.
High-Resolution Images — Professional photographs or branded graphics at a minimum of 1,920 pixels wide. Stock photography is acceptable. Screenshots and low-resolution images will not be used.
Logo — High-resolution logo file (PNG with transparent background is preferred; SVG is also acceptable). If no logo exists, please advise and options can be discussed.
Brand Colors and Fonts — Hexadecimal color codes and font names, if available. Alternatively, provide links to websites whose visual style reflects your brand.
Domain and Hosting Access — Login credentials for your domain registrar and hosting provider, or written notification that you require our Domain Purchase Setup service ($35 one-time fee).
Reference Websites — Three to five websites whose design, layout, or overall aesthetic you find appealing. This is among the most effective methods for communicating your design vision.
Social Media Profiles — Links to all active social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, etc.) to be integrated and linked within the website.
Third-Party Platform Access — Credentials for scheduling software (e.g., Calendly, Acuity), email marketing platforms, or payment processors to be integrated. Accounts must be active and accessible prior to development.

Content Tip: Content Readiness Is the Primary Timeline Factor

Projects that experience delays almost universally do so due to incomplete content submissions, not development pace. Clients who arrive prepared with all items on this checklist consistently experience shorter timelines and smoother project delivery. Please contact us with any questions regarding content preparation.

Revision Policy

Included — Within Scope

  • Layout adjustments within agreed pages
  • Color, font, and spacing modifications
  • Text edits and content replacements
  • Image substitutions
  • Form field adjustments

Billable — Out of Scope

  • Pages added beyond the package limit
  • Features not in the original scope
  • Complete redesigns after written approval
  • Changes requested after final approval
  • Third-party integrations not previously discussed

All revision requests must be submitted in writing by email to contact@wisdomlayers.com. Verbal requests will not be acted upon. Scope changes will be quoted in writing before any additional work begins.

Payment and Financial Terms Summary

Key Financial Terms — Please Review Carefully

  • Deposit (50%): Due upon signing. Work commences only after the deposit is received and confirmed.
  • Deposit is non-refundable once work has commenced. "Work commencement" includes the discovery call, planning sessions, design drafts, or any development activity.
  • Final Payment (50%): Due upon project completion, prior to launch. Source files will not be delivered and the website will not go live until full payment is confirmed.
  • Invoice Due Window: All invoices are due within 7 days of issuance.
  • Late Fee: Payments 14 or more days overdue will incur a $50 per week late fee plus 1.5% monthly interest (18% annually).
  • Payment Disputes: All billing disputes must be reported in writing within 5 business days of invoice receipt.
  • Accepted Methods: PayPal, Money Order, or Cashier's Check (contact us by email for details).

  • Intellectual Property: Upon receipt and clearance of full payment, full ownership of the delivered website files (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and associated assets) transfers to the Client. WisdomLayers retains all rights until that point.
  • Content Responsibility: The Client is solely responsible for the accuracy, legality, and appropriateness of all content provided. WisdomLayers bears no liability for claims arising from Client-supplied content.
  • SEO Disclaimer: WisdomLayers implements industry-standard SEO best practices but cannot guarantee specific search engine rankings, traffic volume, or search results. Rankings are influenced by numerous factors outside our control.
  • Portfolio Rights: WisdomLayers reserves the right to display the completed website in its portfolio unless a written confidentiality arrangement is executed (additional fees apply).
  • Third-Party Services: Hosting, domain renewals, payment processors, and other third-party services are the Client's responsibility after launch unless a retainer agreement is in place.
