Growth Plan

How to grow WisdomLayers from solo to studio — without hiring full-time employees

Internal
01 The Model — Solo to Studio

You don't need employees. You need subcontractors — people you hire per project to handle specific tasks. You land the client, manage the relationship, keep the margin. They do the work you assign them. No payroll, no benefits, no commitment beyond the project.

1
Right Now — Fill the Portfolio
0–3 months
  • Complete the 5 empty portfolio slots on the site — get to 7 total projects
  • Raise prices to updated rates (see Pricing Update doc)
  • Start posting work on LinkedIn — before/after, project breakdowns, behind the scenes
  • Ask Miles2Wisdom and PJ Pressure Washing for referrals and Google reviews
  • Do NOT hire anyone yet — learn your own workflow first
2
First Subcontractor — One Role Only
When you're turning down or delaying 2+ projects/month
  • Logo/graphic design is the first thing to outsource — you're a developer, not a designer
  • Find a designer on Fiverr or Upwork, test them on a small project first
  • You pay ~$50–100, charge client $150–250 — that's your margin
  • You still manage the client, handle revisions, deliver the final files
  • Once that's smooth, add copywriting as the second subcontract role
3
Build a Reliable Roster
When you have 3–4 active/recurring clients
  • Have 2–3 subcontractors you trust and can call on per project: designer, writer, SEO
  • Keep relationships warm — check in monthly even when not actively working together
  • Start packaging their services into your own offerings (you sell it, they deliver it)
  • Consider a junior developer for overflow dev work when you're fully booked
4
Hire First Part-Time Person
When revenue is consistent enough to cover their rate + your profit
  • First hire should be for your most time-consuming repeatable task — likely project management or dev
  • Start part-time (10–15 hrs/week), not full-time
  • Must have documented processes before hiring — if you can't explain it, you can't delegate it
  • Minimum revenue signal: consistently hitting $5,000–$8,000/month before considering this step
02 Where to Find Subcontractors
Fiverr
Best for: Logo, graphics, copywriting
Fixed-price gigs. Fast turnaround. Good for one-off tasks where you know exactly what you need. Filter by reviews and portfolio.
You pay: $30–$150/gig
Upwork
Best for: Developers, SEO, ongoing work
Better for longer-term relationships. You post a job, review proposals, hire. More vetting required but better for complex or recurring work.
You pay: $25–$75/hr
LinkedIn
Best for: Designers, marketers, junior devs
Search "freelance web designer Fayetteville" or browse profiles. Message directly. Great for local collabs and people who want portfolio work.
You pay: Negotiated directly
Discord
Best for: Dev communities, long-term collabs
Join web dev / design Discord servers. Post in #looking-for-work channels. People here want real project experience, not just gig money.
You pay: Negotiated, often low
Local Colleges
Best for: Hungry students, low cost
FTCC, Methodist University, Fayetteville State. Students want portfolio pieces. Offer a small fee + portfolio credit. Great for design and content work.
You pay: $50–$150 + credit
Referrals
Best for: Trusted, pre-vetted people
Ask current clients, other freelancers, and your network. Word of mouth finds the most reliable people. Always your best long-term source.
You pay: Market rate
Rule: Always test a new subcontractor on a small, low-stakes task before putting them on a real client project. One bad experience with a subcontractor can damage a client relationship you worked hard to build.
03 Roles to Fill (In Order)
Graphic / Logo Designer
Priority 1 — First to Outsource
What they do: Logo design, brand colors, icons, basic graphics for the site.

Where to find: Fiverr (search "modern logo design"), check portfolio carefully.

Your margin: Pay $50–100, charge client $150–250.
Copywriter
Priority 2 — Most Clients Need This
What they do: Write page content, service descriptions, About sections, CTAs.

Where to find: Fiverr, Upwork. Look for writers with small business or web copy experience.

Your margin: Pay $30–50/page, charge client $75–125/page.
SEO Specialist
Priority 3 — Add When You Offer Ongoing SEO
What they do: Keyword research, on-page optimization, reporting, link building.

Where to find: Upwork is best for this. Look for proven results and case studies.

Your margin: Pay $150–300/mo, charge client $300–500/mo.
Junior Developer
Priority 4 — When You're Fully Booked
What they do: Handle overflow dev work — HTML/CSS builds, basic JS, updates.

Where to find: Discord dev communities, local colleges, GitHub profiles.

Your margin: Pay $25–40/hr, bill client at your standard rate.
Project Manager / VA
Priority 5 — When Admin Is Eating Your Time
What they do: Client communication, follow-ups, scheduling, sending docs/invoices.

Where to find: Upwork, LinkedIn. Look for someone organized with client experience.

Your margin: Frees your time to take on more projects — indirect ROI.
Photographer / Videographer
Priority 6 — Niche Add-On
What they do: Professional photos for client websites — restaurants, therapists, local biz.

Where to find: Local Fayetteville photographers on Instagram or Google.

Your margin: Refer or package — charge a coordination fee on top.
