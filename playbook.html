Cold Outreach — Initial Contact with a New Prospect

You found a business without a website or with a weak one. Send a short, personalized cold email. Do not attach documents yet.

[Personalized line, e.g., "You've got great reviews but no website to send people to" or "Your current site hasn't been updated in a while and it's probably costing you customers."] A solid website works for you 24/7. It's the thing that makes someone trust you before they ever pick up the phone or walk through your door. I build custom websites for local businesses starting at $499. No templates, no monthly fees. Build Complete — Send for Review

The full website is built. Share the staging link and give the client their 10-business-day review window.

Website Ready for Review Email
Subject: Your Website Is Ready! Here's Your Preview Link
Hey [First Name], It's done. Your website is built and ready for your eyes. Preview link: [STAGING URL] Take your time going through every single page. Content, links, contact forms, how it looks on your phone, all of it. When you're ready to send feedback, compile everything into one email and send it to contact@wisdomlayers.com. That way I can action everything at once and get the updated version back to you quickly. Per our agreement you have 10 business days to submit revision requests. Anything after final approval is billed as new work. A few things I'd recommend checking specifically: Is all the text correct and free of errors? Are your contact details accurate everywhere they appear? Do all the buttons and links work the way you'd expect? Does it look good on your phone? Does the overall look and feel feel right for your brand? Take a good look and let me know what you think. I'm proud of this one. Wisdom Johnson WisdomLayers contact@wisdomlayers.com wisdomlayers.com

Subject: Your Support Period Ends on [DATE]: Here Are Your Options
Hey [First Name], Quick heads up, your post-launch support period wraps up on [DATE], about two weeks from now. I wanted to give you a heads up early so you're not caught off guard. After that date here's how we can keep things moving: Option 1: Pay Per Change Only pay when you need something done. Simple as that. Small change (text, links, minor styling): $50 Medium change (new section, page updates): $150 Large change (new page, new feature): $300+ Option 2: Monthly Maintenance Retainer at $150 per month This gets you 3 small changes per month, priority response time, and a monthly site health check. You can cancel anytime with 30 days notice. Just reply and let me know what direction works best for you. If I don't hear back your account will default to pay per change after the support period ends. It's been a genuine pleasure building this for you. Looking forward to staying in your corner. Wisdom Johnson WisdomLayers contact@wisdomlayers.com wisdomlayers.com
