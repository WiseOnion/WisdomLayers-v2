Our Mission

Who We
Are

The Retired Military Police Association (RMPA), North Carolina Chapter One, is dedicated to fostering unity among active, honorably discharged, and retired Military Police. Founded in 1995 in the Fayetteville and Fort Bragg area, our chapter is one of the oldest and most active in the nation. Our goal is to build a strong community rooted in camaraderie, support, and shared purpose — honoring those who served with distinction in the Military Police Corps.

Strengthen Connections

Build lasting bonds among Military Police members, past and present, across every era of service.

Promote Friendship & Respect

A culture of mutual support, dignity, and brotherhood across all backgrounds and service branches.

Support Law Enforcement

Standing behind active and retired Military Police and the broader civilian law enforcement community.

Honor the Legacy

Enhancing the recognition and reputation of the Military Police Corps and those who served with distinction.

What We Stand For

Our Core Pillars

01

Brotherhood & Camaraderie

Forge lifetime bonds with those who wore the MP brassard. Our shared experiences and sacrifice create connections that transcend time and rank.

02

Veteran Support

Advocate for the needs of our veteran community. From VA benefits guidance to peer support networks, we are here for one another every step of the way.

03

Community Service

Give back to the Fayetteville community that has long been home to Fort Bragg and the Military Police Corps. Service does not end at retirement.

04

Honoring Service

Preserve and celebrate the proud history of the Military Police Corps. We memorialize those who gave the ultimate sacrifice and honor all who served.

Our History

Chapter One

RMPA Chapter One was established in 1995, born from the deep roots of the Military Police community in the Fayetteville and Fort Bragg region of North Carolina. As Chapter One of the national Retired Military Police Association, we hold the distinction of being one of the founding chapters and a model for chapters across the country.

Inclusivity Policy

Open to All

RMPA Chapter One is proud to be an inclusive organization. Membership is open to all individuals who have served honorably in the Military Police Corps — regardless of background.

Our Commitment

Membership in RMPA Chapter One is open to all active duty, honorably discharged, and retired Military Police regardless of race, color, sex, religion, age, disability, or national origin. We believe that every person who served the Corps deserves a community, and every community is stronger for its diversity. Our doors are open — always.

Eligibility
  • Active duty Military Police personnel
  • Honorably discharged Military Police veterans
  • Retired Military Police of any rank or branch
  • Supporters and associates of the MP community
